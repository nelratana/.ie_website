Our Work

Why Cambodia?

Today in Cambodia there is a learning crisis. The Khmer Rouge Regime killed over 90% of teachers and the education system was completely destroyed. Findings from the 2018 MoEYS OECD Report reveal less than 3% of 15 year old children reach base line levels of performance in Maths or Literacy. The future for many Cambodian children is bleak.

SeeBeyondBorders is founded on the belief that change begins with education and that good quality schooling allows Cambodian children to seek a brighter future.

Overview

SeeBeyondBorders Ireland was established to build bridges between Cambodia and Ireland. SeeBeyondBorders Ireland will focus on furthering the connections and support that have been made on the Island of Ireland by the Irish Nationals who have been connected with education work in Cambodia. Now is the time to consolidate and strengthen that Irish voice and to give it the specific recognition it has earned and allow it to grow further. Ireland as a nation has a particular connection with Cambodia, with both countries having similarities in their respective histories and similarities in their respective relationships with their own continents, looking to protect and promote their own cultural identities. SeeBeyondBorders Ireland is a development organisation as opposed to a provider of welfare.

In Ireland

Our objectives are reflected in the broader SeeBeyondBorders Strategic Plan in reference to its development activities that take place in Cambodia which are to:

a) Improve childrens learning outcomes by focussing on teaching practice in early years and in particularly on developing childrens maths and literacy skills.
b) Become a recognised centre of excellence whereby the organisation becomes one of the go to specialists in its core areas of early grade learning.
c) Grow the organisation so as to secure both the staff and funding needed to deliver on its objectives.

SeeBeyondBorders Ireland will work as a support to the direct work being conducted in Cambodia but also function efficiently and effectively as our own organisation in Ireland.
